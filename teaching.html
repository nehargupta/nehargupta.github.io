Women in Tech Related

I am passionate about mentoring and teaching women in tech.

South Norwalk Girls Who Code Club

From 2017 to 2018, I o Taught introductory Python to a group of 20 of middle school girls on a weekly basis o Encouraged career outlook by presenting and facilitating a dialogue about industry leaders o Curriculum advisor to national nonprofit, promoting project-based learning in course materials o Connected with Michigan State University and Stamford, CT organizations to advise with their club launch I had the chance to speak about these experiences at the PyCon Education Summit at PyCon in Cleveland, OH: This was a really enjoyable experience for me, and I also had the chance to learn a lot from a number of amazing people also passionate about women's education and women in STEM, including Dr. Cynthia Barnett: https://www.amazinggirlsscience.com and Vanessa Liles, and Jessica Jahnke, and more.

ACM-W Chapter Officer

At Michigan State University “Women in Computing" club has roughly 40-50 members total. I was President from 2015 – 2016 and Treasurer from 2013 – 2015. I implemented new leadership development, and confidence building programs, using Lean In activities. Our attendance at national conferences increased by over 40% under my presidency. We also doubled our community service activities and local K-12 outreach, and won several Google and National Counsel of Women in Technology (NCWIT) grants for community service I'm honored to say I won a few scholarships for my work with this organization, including the MSU College of Engineering Service Award, Feb 2015 Michigan Council of Women in Technology (MCWT) Scholarship, the MSU Women in Computing “Extra Mile” Leadership Award, the MSU Women in Engineering Award, the Google Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing Travel Grant, and the Anita Borg Scholarship for Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.
